Pixel Jam 2014 happened! We got together on the 12th of September to have a crack at making a whole video game in just 48 hours.

About

Pixel Jam is open to anyone who wants to test their mettle by getting just a weekend to put together a whole video game. You can enter as an individual or as a team, and you'll need be able to build the design, code and art all in the 48 hours you're given.

The competition begins at 6:00 PM on Friday the 12th of September at Victoria University in Wellington, and will end at 6:00 PM the following Sunday. There'll be a common theme that everyone has to build their game around, but otherwise there's no limits to what you can make: 2D, 3D, FPS or RPG, it's all open.

There'll be an entry fee of $30, and we supply T-shirts and some food. And there are prizes up for grabs! With judges from the local game development industry it's also a great way to get yourself out there and get recognised.

You can email me at tim@pxljm.com for more information, or check out the Facebook page.

FAQ

  • How much can we prepare beforehand?

    You can use existing frameworks, development tools and even your own libraries. The main thing is that all the code and artwork specific to the actual game are produced during the event.

  • Do we need to bring our own hardware?

    Yes, you'll need to bring your own computers and any other equipment you want to use. You can safely leave this equipment at the venue overnight. We'll supply power and internet.

  • Can we come with a game idea already in mind?

    We're not opposed to this, but it'd be great if everyone opened with a game design session. The theme is there in part to counter you bringing a detailed design with you, but a general idea of what you want to make isn't a bad thing.

  • Do we have 24/7 access to the venue? Are we allowed to leave?

    We're still working this out. It looks like we may be able to run overnight access, but we may have to restrict access to leaving and arriving during the night.

  • What's with the fee? Is it per person or per team?

    The fee is per person, to pay for the shirts and food. Bring it with you to the event and we'll get it off you once we hand out the shirts.

  • Where is it, exactly?

    On the first floor of the Alan MacDiarmid (AM) building at the VUW Kelburn campus. You can find it near the corner of Kelburn Parade and Glasgow Street, with a Wishbone by the entrance. This map should help you find it. We'll have signs up to direct you to the room once you're there.

  • Is there parking available?

    Parking is available over the weekend.
